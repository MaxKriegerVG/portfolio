CROSSNIQ

November 2017
Browser/PC/Mac/Linux
Unity


CROSSNIQ is a free "y2k aesthetic" arcade-style puzzle game I developed in my spare time while working as a Salesforce developer in 2017.

Drawing from games like Yoshi's Cookie, Tetris, and a Rubik's Cube, CROSSNIQ focuses on simple rules and fast play. Its popularity led me to crowdfund a more fully realized version of the game, CROSSNIQ+.

You can either download or play CROSSNIQ in your browser at this link.

I'm a game developer, graphic designer, and multimedia artist based in Cleveland, OH - but I could be coming to your town, if you'd like.

I graduated from DePaul University's CDM in 2015 with a BS in Video Game Development. Since then, I've created VR/AR installations, web games, narrative games, and even made the jump to full-time indie developer for my first console title, CROSSNIQ+ (Switch).

Right now, I'm looking to hang up the indie mantle for a while and work in a game dev environment where I can grow, collaborate, and ultimately help build something great. If you know a place like that, we should talk!
